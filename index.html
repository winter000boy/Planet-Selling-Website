Mercury

Mercury is the first planet from the Sun and the smallest in the Solar System. It is named after the Roman god Mercurius, god of commerce and communication, and the messenger of the gods.

Venus

Venus, the second planet from the Sun, is the hottest in the Solar System due to its thick atmosphere of carbon dioxide and clouds of sulfuric acid.

Earth

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only planet known to support life. Its surface is 71% water, making it unique among the planets.

Mars

Mars, the "Red Planet," is the fourth from the Sun and is known for its dusty, iron-oxide-rich surface and towering volcanoes.

Jupiter

Jupiter, the fifth planet from the Sun, is the largest in the Solar System. Its atmosphere is dominated by hydrogen and helium, and it features the Great Red Spot, a massive storm.

Saturn

Saturn, the sixth planet from the Sun, is best known for its prominent ring system, made up of ice, rock, and dust.

Uranus

Uranus, the seventh planet, is a gas giant with a pale blue-green hue due to methane in its atmosphere. It rotates on its side, unlike any other planet.

Neptune

Neptune, the eighth planet from the Sun, is a deep blue gas giant with the fastest winds recorded in the Solar System.
